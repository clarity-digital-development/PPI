## 6. Buyer/Seller-Side Services + RESPA Reality Check

**Critical finding — RESPA constraint:**
- Federal **Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act** prohibits home warranty companies, title companies, inspectors, and other "settlement service" providers from paying compensation/kickbacks to real estate agents or brokers for referrals. [First American terms explicitly exclude agents](https://refer.fahw.com/zone/terms?journey.campaign_id=7195938458732519808). [2-10.com](https://www.2-10.com/blog/why-real-estate-agents-should-partner-with-home-warranty-companies/)
- **Implication**: PPI is NOT a settlement service provider. PPI is a sign install company. PPI is NOT a real estate agent. Therefore PPI introducing a *seller* (not the agent) to a home warranty / inspector / moving vendor via the "your sign just went up" trigger email is **likely legal where the agent kickback would not be**. Tanner needs a real estate attorney to confirm before launching — but the gap is real.

**Vendors PPI could affiliate with:**
- **Moving**: Bellhop has an explicit real estate agent referral program with team-match + photo intros. PODS partners with Bellhop for container storage. [getbellhops.com/agent](https://www.getbellhops.com/agent/) Updater (also worth a search) is the dominant move-coordination platform sold to brokerages.
- **Home warranty**: 2-10 HBW, AHS, First American all have real-estate-pro programs (typically structured as "free coverage for the listing period" rather than cash kickback, to stay RESPA-compliant). [2-10.com/brokers-agents](https://www.2-10.com/brokers-agents/coverage-your-clients-deserve/) [ahs.com/real-estate-pros](https://www.ahs.com/real-estate-pros/)
- **Inspection, cleaning, staging, junk removal** — fragmented local markets; PPI could resell as marketplace.

**Shape for PPI**: post-install transactional email to the seller ("your home is now on the market — book a moving quote, get a home warranty quote, schedule cleaning") with affiliate links. Lead-share or CPA payment from vendors. PPI is the introducer, not the agent, so RESPA likely doesn't bite — but legal review required.
