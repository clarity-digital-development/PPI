## Framing before the list

The baseline data changes how to think about "net-new revenue." PPI is doing **~$3,400/month in net revenue** with **labor at ~96% of revenue** even with optimistic routing. Net-new ideas must be evaluated against two filters:

1. **Does it raise AOV on an order we're already running?** (zero marginal drive cost)
2. **Does it create recurring revenue not tied to a truck visit?** (pure margin)

Ideas that require *additional* truck visits (drone, photos, yard touch-up) are net-negative unless priced like a contractor visit. The current labor math doesn't support adding service complexity at PPI's wages.

## TOP 3 — what Ryan should pursue first

### **#1: Design Your Sign (build it, but stage it after #5)**

Already scoped (`docs/design-your-sign/plan.md`). The economics are unique to this idea: **~80% gross margin on a $129 product with no incremental drive time** (the sign rides along on the existing install). Break-even at 30 signs ≈ achievable inside a quarter if pitched to the 20 anchor customers (top-20 = 78% of revenue, they trust PPI, they all have signs they hate).

**Why it's #1:** It's the only idea in the list that materially changes the AOV math without changing the ops model. June AOV already jumped from $54 → $76 just by killing the TOOCOLD promo; adding Design Your Sign to even **20% of orders at +$70 net** ($129 retail − $59 they'd have paid for the standard post-only) pushes blended AOV past $90. That changes the labor-as-%-of-revenue math from 96% to ~75% — the first genuinely breakeven number in this analysis.

**Watchout:** Ryan can't ship this in a week. While it's building, ship #5 + #2 (below) for immediate revenue.

### **#2: Restructure the sign rental fee (1-week ship, biggest immediate lift)**

The current schedule (free 6mo, $18 at 6mo, $18 at 9mo, $6/mo after 12mo) was built for a world where signs stay in-ground for months. **The data says median install lifetime is 35 days.** Of 138 active installs, only 4 have crossed 90 days, none have crossed 180. **The entire rental fee structure is collecting $0 because nobody hits the cliff.**

**Two restructures to evaluate:**
- **Option A (lower friction):** $5/week starting week 5 (gives the same 30-day grace, then meters). 35-day median = ~$5 extra per order at zero ops cost. ~$2,500/year at current volume; scales linearly with volume.
- **Option B (clean):** Raise the base post install fee from $59 → $69 and call rental "included for 60 days, $10/wk after." Customers prefer one number; PPI gets a real revenue floor.

**Why this is the highest-ROI ops change in the brief:** zero new infrastructure, fixes a $0-revenue line item, ships in days.

### **#3: Lockbox + QR Code attach push at checkout**

Two low-effort, ship-this-week additions that compound:

- **Lockbox attach is 13%.** Lockboxes are inventory PPI already owns. Even modest UI re-prompting at checkout ("Add a lockbox install — $25" with one-click yes) realistically hits 25-30% attach. At +$20/order revenue × 30% incremental attach × 800 orders/year run-rate = **$4,800/year of pure margin** (no extra drive, lockbox is grabbed off the truck).
- **QR codes printed on riders/signs** (per the Design Your Sign research, agents want "Scan for home value" CTAs). $10-15 SKU added to the rider lineup. Riders attach 33.9% today — adding a "QR rider +$12" option lifts that line meaningfully without new ops.

**Why bundled:** both are pure checkout-UX changes. Same week of work, same deployment, both hit the same flow. Combined annual upside at current volume: **~$6-8K**, scaling with volume.

## What this brief implies for Ryan's strategy

Ryan's current thesis is "push volume." The data says **volume alone won't fix this** because every order at $54-76 AOV is structurally a labor loss. The actual sequence is:

1. **Week 1-2:** Ship rental restructure (#5) + checkout attach push (#2/#3). Buys $5-10K/yr.

The big honest reframe: PPI doesn't have a margin problem caused by drive time and wages. **PPI has an AOV problem.** Every recommendation above is in service of moving AOV from ~$65 to ~$90, after which the existing cost structure becomes survivable.
